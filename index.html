About Me

I'm currently a student at the University of California, Berkeley, majoring in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology.

I've always had a passion for scientific learning and innovation. This carries over into my desire to push the boundaries of current knowledge about ourselves and the world around us through academic research.

My hope is that this knowledge will allow me to design solutions to today's persistent issues in medicine and healthcare, create new devices to gather even more knowledge, and to satisfy my burning curiosity.

Experience

I've worked at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in the lab of Dr. Catherine Murphy to characterize drug loading and delivery via mesoporous silica coated gold nanorods.

I've worked at the Boston University in the lab of Dr. Tyrone Porter to characterize the drug loading and release dynamics of doxorubicin within lipid-polymer hybrid nanoparticles.

I've worked at Henlius Biopharmaceuticals to assist them in developing a more efficient way to perform antibody screening by improving upon multiplexed fluorescent cell barcoding.
